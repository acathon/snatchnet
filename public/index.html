Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SnatchNet

Is SnatchNet completely free?

Yes, 100% free. No account, no subscription, no hidden fees. Just paste a YouTube link and download — no software to install.

What video resolutions are available?

Up to 4K (2160p), 1080p Full HD, 720p HD, 480p, and 360p — all as MP4. Available resolutions depend on what the creator originally uploaded.

Can I download audio as MP3?

Yes! Switch to the Audio tab and choose MP3 Audio for a 192 kbps MP3 file (converted server-side via ffmpeg). You can also grab the native M4A Audio for best quality.

What's the difference between MP3 and M4A?

M4A is YouTube's native audio format — higher quality at a smaller file size, ideal for Apple devices and quality-focused listening. MP3 is the universal format supported by every device and music player, making it the most compatible choice.

Is downloading YouTube videos legal?

SnatchNet is designed for personal, offline use only. Downloading content you own or that is openly licensed is generally permitted. Commercial redistribution or sharing copyrighted content without permission is not allowed. Always respect creators and applicable law.

Is my data private and secure?

We don't collect personal data, use cookies, or track you. URLs you submit are processed in real time and never stored. IP addresses are used only for rate limiting. Downloaded files are deleted from our servers immediately after delivery.

Why does the file open in my browser instead of downloading?

Some browsers choose to play back media instead of saving it. If that happens, right-click the media and select Save video as… or Save audio as… to save it to your device.

Which YouTube link formats are supported?

Standard watch links (youtube.com/watch?v=), short links (youtu.be/), and YouTube Shorts (youtube.com/shorts/) are all supported.

Why is my download taking so long?

HD and 4K videos require the server to download and merge separate video & audio streams (DASH), which takes 20–60 seconds for longer content. MP3 conversion adds a few extra seconds for ffmpeg processing. Choosing a lower resolution or 360p (which has a pre-merged stream) is noticeably faster.

Is there a download limit?

We apply fair-use rate limits: 30 video look-ups per minute and 5 downloads per minute per IP address. These limits reset every minute and are more than enough for normal personal use.
